# Biography: Jerry Shane Sanderson

In the late summer of 1983, the story of Jerry Shane Sanderson began in the city of Ardmore, Oklahoma. Born on August 31 to Freddie Ray Sanderson Jr. and Pearl Anne Sanderson—a woman affectionately known to her kin as "Mossie"—Jerry entered a world defined by the deep familial roots of the Sooner State.

Jerry grew up as part of a vibrant and expansive family tapestry. His life was shared with a close-knit circle of siblings: his sisters, Jennifer Beyer and Stephanie VanCuren, and his brothers, Randall Ray Sanderson and Donnie Hogan, the latter of whom was known to the family by the enduring moniker "Judge." Within this lively household, the bonds of brotherhood and sisterhood were forged, creating a foundation that would follow Jerry throughout his years.

As he moved into adulthood, the focus of Jerry’s life shifted toward the legacy he would leave behind. He became a father to two daughters, Madisyn Paige Sanderson and Marley Peyton Sanderson. These children represented the continuation of the Sanderson line, a new generation born from the history of Ardmore and the values passed down from Freddie and Mossie.

Though his journey eventually led him to the quiet community of Wilson, Oklahoma, where he would ultimately find his rest, the narrative of Jerry Shane Sanderson remains one of family and place. He is remembered not merely through the dates of a ledger, but as a son of Oklahoma, a brother, and a father whose story lives on through the lives of Madisyn and Marley.
